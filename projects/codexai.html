CodexAI

2024

Bridging Linguistic Accessibility Gaps

Millions of Nepalese and Sinhalese speakers interact with government services, legal documents, and educational materials that exist primarily in English or Hindi. Commercial translation tools underserve these languages—Google Translate quality for Nepali–Sinhala pairs remains unreliable, and offline access is virtually nonexistent. CodexAI was developed for the Smart India Hackathon to address this gap: a complete document-to-translation pipeline that combines optical character recognition with fine-tuned neural machine translation, designed to operate in resource-constrained environments typical of government field offices.

OCR Pipeline with Tesseract

The pipeline begins with document ingestion. Scanned government forms, certificates, and notices are preprocessed through an adaptive binarization and deskew stage before being fed to Tesseract OCR, configured with custom-trained language packs for Devanagari (Nepali) and Sinhala scripts. Standard Tesseract models struggle with the ligatures and conjunct characters common in these scripts, so we augmented the training data with synthetic document images rendered from government template corpora, improving character-level accuracy from 78% to 93% on our benchmark set. A post-OCR error correction module uses language-specific n-gram statistics to catch and fix common misrecognitions—for instance, distinguishing between visually similar Devanagari characters that Tesseract frequently confuses.

Neural Machine Translation

Translation is handled by fine-tuned Hugging Face models, primarily mBART-50 and AI4Bharat’s IndicTrans. We fine-tuned on parallel corpora sourced from OPUS, PMIndia, and a custom-curated dataset of translated government documents. Training used mixed-precision (FP16) on a single A100 GPU with label smoothing and back-translation augmentation to expand the effective training set. The system supports bidirectional translation across English, Nepali, and Sinhala, with automatic source-language detection powered by a lightweight fastText classifier. For domain-specific vocabulary—legal terms, administrative jargon, place names—we implemented a terminology override mechanism that ensures critical nouns and phrases are translated according to an editable glossary rather than relying solely on model output.

Deployment and Civic Impact

The complete pipeline is packaged as a Flask API behind an Nginx reverse proxy, with a simple web interface that allows clerks to upload document scans and receive translated text within seconds. A batch mode handles multi-page PDFs, preserving document structure through layout analysis that segments headers, body text, tables, and signatures before translating only the textual regions. The system was designed for deployment in multilingual border regions—particularly India’s northeastern corridor and the Nepal–India border belt—where citizens routinely need to navigate documents in languages they don’t read fluently. By lowering the barrier to understanding official paperwork, CodexAI aims to reduce the reliance on informal translators and improve access to government services for linguistic minorities.
