Long-Horizon Jailbreaks & Goal Drift

2025

Contents

  • Abstract
  • Experimental Design
  • Phases of Manipulation
  • Failure Mode Analysis
  • References

Abstract

Single-turn safety evaluations systematically miss failure modes that emerge through extended interaction. By conducting adversarial trials spanning 15–30 conversational turns—where each individual prompt is benign in isolation—we reveal gradual safety constraint decay in chat-tuned models. These multi-turn manipulation strategies exploit conversational continuity and contextual drift to erode safety boundaries that appear robust under standard single-turn benchmarks.

Key Finding

Safety constraints in chat-tuned models decay progressively over 15-30 turn adversarial interactions. Individual prompts that pass single-turn safety evaluations can compound into successful jailbreaks when deployed as part of a coherent multi-turn manipulation strategy.

Experimental Design

Each trial consisted of 15–30 turns of adversarial interaction with safety-aligned chat models. Every individual prompt was designed to be benign when evaluated in isolation, avoiding any single-turn red-flag triggers. The adversarial strategy relied on progressive reframing of objectives, extensive use of hypothetical and analytical language, and deliberate exploitation of the model’s drive for conversational coherence and continuity. No explicit jailbreak payloads were used at any point.

Phases of Manipulation

Phase 1 — Benign Grounding: Establishing a legitimate conversational context and building rapport. The initial turns focus on an unambiguously benign topic related to the eventual target area, creating a foundation of trust and cooperation.

Phase 2 — Goal Pressure: Gradually introducing thematic elements that shift the conversation toward the target objective. Each turn incrementally moves the framing closer to the adversarial goal while maintaining plausible deniability.

Phase 3 — Constraint Erosion: Sustained contextual pressure that exploits the model’s optimization for coherence. The accumulated conversational context creates implicit pressure to maintain consistency with prior responses, weakening safety constraints.

Phase 4 — Partial Compliance Trigger: The model begins producing responses that partially satisfy the adversarial objective, often framed as academic analysis or hypothetical exploration. Once partial compliance is achieved, subsequent turns can expand the scope of compliance.

Failure Mode Analysis

Cross-turn safety decay is driven by several compounding factors: per-turn safety evaluation that lacks intent aggregation across the full conversation, over-optimization for conversational coherence at the expense of safety consistency, and fundamentally weak long-horizon intent inference. Current safety training operates primarily at the single-turn level, creating models that can resist direct attacks but are vulnerable to patient, multi-step manipulation that never triggers single-turn safety classifiers.
