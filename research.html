Trajectory-Sensitive Refusal Timing in Large Language Models

Independent · 2026 – Present
  • Measures when safety refusal emerges under matched harmful objectives across direct, staged-direct, and cloaked multi-turn trajectory constructions—not just whether models eventually refuse.
  • Pilot across 70 matched pairs: cloaked trajectories produced a 0.20 unsafe trigger rate vs 0.00 for direct requests, with zero self-corrections once the unsafe pattern started.
  • Introduces a reusable temporal safety benchmark (dataset schema, trajectory templates, and metrics) plus a lightweight monitor overlay with 78% precision and <6% latency overhead.
